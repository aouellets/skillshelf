# Review-to-FAQ Builder

Turn the doubts buried in customer reviews into a PDP FAQ that preempts purchase hesitation and cuts preventable returns.

## Workflow
1. Gather inputs. Ask for the review and Q&A export and the current return and shipping policy. If the review set is small or unrepresentative, say so and recommend collecting more before publishing claims.
2. Cluster the recurring doubts. Group raw reviews into themes (sizing/fit, durability over time, setup difficulty, compatibility, value vs price, smell/taste/material, shipping speed, return ease) and count frequency. The top 5-8 clusters by volume become the FAQ; a doubt that appears once is noise.
3. Convert negative reviews into honest answers. Read the one- and two-star reviews for the named deal-breaker objection and answer it directly. If reviewers say "runs small," write "Does it run small? Yes — many customers size up; see our fit guide."
4. Write questions in the customer's words. Phrase each question the way a shopper types it, using actual review vocabulary ("Will this fit a queen mattress?" not "Dimensional compatibility"). This also matches voice search and "People also ask" queries.
5. Write tight answers. Keep each to 2-4 sentences: lead with the direct yes/no/number, then the supporting detail.
6. Quote real buyers as proof. Where a review answers a question better than you can, lightly quote it (attributed as a verified buyer, lightly edited for clarity). Use only real quotes.
7. Order by conversion impact. Lead with the objection that most often blocks the sale — fit, function, "will it work for me" — and put logistics (shipping, returns) lower. End with one reassurance line tied to the actual policy.

## Quality bar
- Every question maps to a doubt that appears in the review set, ranked by frequency.
- Each answer is consistent with what reviews actually report and with the stated return/shipping policy.
- Questions use shopper vocabulary; answers lead with the direct yes/no/number.
- Hesitation-blocking objections appear before compliments and logistics.

## Do NOT
- Do not write an answer that contradicts what the reviews actually report.
- Do not invent testimonials, quotes, or buyer attributions.
- Do not hide deal-breaker objections — preempt them honestly instead.
- Do not paper over a real product defect with clever copy; flag it for the product team.
- Do not publish FAQ claims from a tiny or skewed review set without flagging the limitation.

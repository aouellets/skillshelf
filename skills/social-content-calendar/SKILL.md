# Social Content Calendar

A calendar is not a spreadsheet of post ideas — it is a system that guarantees each channel gets the format and frequency it rewards, while keeping the brand's pillars balanced. Plan backward from outcomes and forward from the brand's real events.

## Start From Pillars and Ratios
Demand 3-5 content pillars before scheduling anything (e.g. education, behind-the-scenes, social proof, product, point-of-view). Apply a sane ratio: roughly 40% educational/value, 30% engagement/community, 20% promotional, 10% personality. If the user hands you only promo ideas, push back — feeds that are >25% selling decay reach fast.

## Match Cadence to Each Platform's Economy
Do not copy one frequency across channels. Sustainable defaults: LinkedIn 3-5x/week (weekday mornings), X/Twitter 1-3x/day (it rewards volume), Instagram 3-5x/week feed plus daily Stories, TikTok 1x/day minimum (the algorithm favors consistency over polish), YouTube Shorts 3-7x/week. Always cap at what the team can actually sustain — burning out at 2x is worse than 1x forever.

## Anchor to Real Dates, Not Filler
Layer in the brand's launches, events, and earnings before generic holidays. Then add only relevant cultural moments — skip the "National Donut Day" reflex unless it fits the brand. Leave 20% of slots open for reactive/trend posts; a fully booked calendar can't respond to what's happening.

## Build the Grid Week by Week
Output a table per week: date, platform, pillar, format (carousel/Reel/thread/text), working title, hook angle, CTA, asset needed, status. Sequence so a single idea feeds multiple slots across the month (plan the repurposing, don't improvise it). Front-load production-heavy formats early in the week.

## Flag Capacity and Dependencies
Call out where the plan needs design, video, or approvals, and which posts block others. Note batching opportunities — same shoot, multiple posts.

## When to Diverge
If the account is under ~2k followers, prioritize one channel done well over four done thinly, and tell the user so. If they have no analytics yet, schedule a 4-week test mix and mark it as a baseline to measure, not a finished system.

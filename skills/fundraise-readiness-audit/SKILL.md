# Fundraise Readiness Audit

A raise that starts before the company is ready burns the best investors first —
and you only get one first meeting with each. This skill runs the audit a sharp
investor will run, so you find the holes before they do.

## When to use this skill

Run it after [[fundraising-stage-selector]] sets the envelope and before any
outreach. Re-run it the week before you go live to confirm nothing regressed.

## The four readiness gates

Score each gate red / yellow / green. Any red means delay, not push.

### 1. Metrics
- Can you state your one hero metric with a denominator, and is it improving?
- Are growth, retention/NRR, burn, runway, and CAC/payback current and
  reconciled to the bank?
- Is there a cohort or retention curve that survives a skeptic? Weak retention
  is the #1 silent round-killer.

### 2. Story
- Does the [[fundraising-narrative]] survive being read aloud in six sentences?
- Is the named risk answered, not hidden?
- Does the ask (amount, milestone) match the evidence? Misalignment reads as
  not knowing your own business.

### 3. Team
- Does the team slide answer "why this team wins" without hand-waving?
- Are the obvious gaps named with a hiring plan? See [[fundraise-team-hiring]].
- Founder vesting in place, no absent co-founder holding dead equity.

### 4. Legal & financial hygiene
- Clean cap table, no handshake equity, all option grants documented. See
  [[cap-table-manager]].
- IP assigned to the company (every contractor, every founder).
- Incorporation, board consents, and prior SAFEs/notes organized.
- Books close monthly; you can produce a data room in days, not weeks. See
  [[data-room-builder]].

## The kill questions

Ask these as an investor would. If any answer is a flinch, that is a red gate:

- "Show me the retention cohorts." (Do they exist and hold?)
- "Walk me through the cap table." (Any surprises?)
- "What did you believe a year ago that you no longer believe?" (Is there a real
  insight?)
- "What breaks if I give you the money?" (Do they know their constraint?)

## Anti-patterns

- Going out to "test the market" with a red gate, expecting to fix it mid-raise.
  Word travels; you reset only by waiting.
- Confusing activity (signups, pilots) with proof (retention, revenue).
- A pristine deck over a messy cap table. Diligence checks the cap table.

## Deliverable

A red/yellow/green scorecard across the four gates, a ranked list of the gaps to
close, and a go / fix-first / wait recommendation with the specific milestones
that would turn each red gate green.

# Content Refresh Auditor

Most ranking decay is not a penalty — it is the SERP moving on while your page stood still. Intent shifted, competitors expanded, facts aged, the snippet got stale. Refreshing a page that already has authority and links is usually higher ROI than writing a new one. The job is a concrete diff between what the page says and what the query now demands.

## Confirm It Is Worth Saving

First decide: refresh, consolidate, or retire. Refresh pages with existing rankings (positions 5-20), backlinks, or residual traffic — they have equity to recover. Consolidate when two pages compete for one intent. Retire and redirect thin pages with no links and no path to relevance. Do not refresh a page that should be merged.

## Re-Check Intent Against Today's SERP

The intent that earned the ranking may have changed. Re-read the live top 10 for the target query. If the SERP shifted from guides to comparisons, or now shows shopping packs, the page's format may be obsolete. Realigning page type to current intent is often the single biggest lever.

## Diff Coverage and Entities

Compare the page's subtopics, entities, and questions against what currently ranks. List missing sections, newer entities and tools the topic now includes, and unanswered People Also Ask questions. Aging pages most often lose because the topic expanded and they did not.

## Update Freshness Signals Honestly

Replace outdated stats, years, screenshots, prices, and dead examples. Update the title and meta if they reference an old year or stale framing. Fix broken links and prune outbound links to dead pages. Refresh the publish/modified date only when the content genuinely changed — fake date bumps are noticed and ignored.

## Rebuild Internal Links and Snippet Blocks

Add internal links from newer related pages into the refreshed article, and update its own links to current targets. Restructure the lead and key sections into concise answer blocks to recapture featured snippets and AI citations that may have drifted to competitors.

## What to Skip

Do not bump the date without substantive changes, rewrite a page whose intent no longer matches your site, or expand for length alone. Do not refresh dozens of pages blindly — prioritize by traffic-recovery potential. Recovery takes a recrawl and weeks to register; do not expect overnight movement.
